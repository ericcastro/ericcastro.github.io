---
title: "Blog Post: Why Personal Sites Should Be Weird Again"
date: February 1998
category: Hot Takes
---
The modern web got too smooth, too clean, and too allergic to personality. In the 90s your homepage could have stars, bevels, MIDI ambitions, and five clashing ideas fighting for screen space. That chaos was part of the charm.

I still like interfaces that feel tactile and alive. I build software, electronics, music-related experiments, and random visual toys, and I prefer a site that feels like a place rather than a card with three muted gradients.
